Dental Implants

The gold standard for permanent tooth replacement — a titanium post that bonds with your jawbone to hold a crown that looks, feels and functions exactly like a natural tooth.

Overview

What Are Dental Implants?

A dental implant is a small titanium screw surgically placed into the jawbone to replace the root of a missing tooth. Over a period of 3–6 months, the implant undergoes osseointegration — a biological process where living bone cells grow around and fuse with the titanium surface, creating an exceptionally stable foundation.

Once integrated, an abutment (connector piece) and a custom-crafted porcelain or zirconia crown are attached to the top. The result is a replacement tooth that is indistinguishable from your natural teeth — in appearance, feel, biting strength and function.

At Grace Dental Care, we use CBCT 3D scanning to plan every implant placement with precision, minimising surgical risk and maximising long-term success rates. Our implant success rate exceeds 98%.

Signs You May Need Implants

If you identify with any of the following, a dental implant consultation is strongly recommended.

Shifting Neighbouring TeethAdjacent teeth drifting into the gap left by a missing tooth.

The Process

Step-by-Step Implant Journey

From your first consultation to your permanent smile — here is exactly what to expect at every stage.

1
Comprehensive Consultation & 3D CBCT Scan
Our implantologist evaluates your oral health, bone density and gum condition. A CBCT 3D scan maps your jawbone precisely, allowing us to plan implant position, angle and depth digitally before any surgery.
⏱ 60–90 minutes · Visit 1
2
Implant Placement Surgery
Under local anaesthesia, a small titanium post is precisely placed into the jawbone at the planned position. The gum is sutured and a healing cap is placed. Most patients report minimal discomfort — comparable to a tooth extraction.
⏱ 45–75 minutes · Visit 2
3
Osseointegration (Healing Phase)
The implant bonds with the surrounding bone through osseointegration — a natural biological process. This critical phase takes 8–12 weeks and is why implants are so durable. During this time you wear a temporary crown.
⏱ 8–12 weeks recovery at home
4
Abutment Placement & Crown Impressions
Once healed, an abutment (connector piece) is attached to the implant. Digital impressions of your teeth are taken and sent to our lab to fabricate a custom crown that precisely matches your bite and tooth shade.
⏱ 30 minutes · Visit 3
5
Permanent Crown Fitting & Bite Adjustment
Your custom zirconia or porcelain crown is attached and your bite is carefully adjusted for perfect comfort. You leave with a fully functional, natural-looking permanent tooth. Final polishing is done chairside.
⏱ 45 minutes · Visit 4

Eligibility

Am I a Candidate?

Ideal Candidates

  • Adults with one or more missing teeth
  • Adequate jawbone density (or after bone graft)
  • Healthy gum tissue with no active periodontal disease
  • Non-smokers or willing to quit during healing
  • Medically fit adults (18+ with fully developed jaw)
  • Those dissatisfied with removable dentures or bridges

May Not Be Suitable If…

  • Uncontrolled diabetes or blood disorders
  • Heavy smokers (significantly reduces success rate)
  • Severe bone loss (unless bone grafting is done first)
  • Children under 18 (jaw still developing)
  • Patients on bisphosphonate medications
  • Active gum disease (must be treated first)

Recovery

Aftercare & Recovery Tips

Following these instructions carefully will maximise your implant's success rate and comfort during healing.

1
Apply Ice PackFor the first 24 hours, apply an ice pack to the cheek in 20-minute intervals to reduce swelling.
2
Eat Soft FoodsStick to yoghurt, idli, mashed rice and soft foods for the first 2 weeks to avoid disturbing the implant site.
3
No SmokingAvoid smoking for at least 6–8 weeks post-surgery. Nicotine impairs blood flow and dramatically reduces success rates.
4
Gentle BrushingBrush carefully around the implant site with a soft-bristled brush. Use a Waterpik for cleaning around the implant base.
5
Take Prescribed MedicationComplete the full course of antibiotics and anti-inflammatories as prescribed to prevent infection.
6
6-Monthly Check-upsSchedule professional cleanings and implant checks every 6 months to ensure long-term health and longevity.
