Pediatric Dentistry

Overview

About Pediatric Dentistry

Pediatric dentistry focuses on the oral health of children from infancy through their teenage years. Milk teeth (deciduous teeth) are essential for chewing, speaking, and holding space for permanent teeth. Early cavities in milk teeth can damage the developing permanent teeth underneath. Pediatric dental care emphasizes prevention through dental sealants, fluoride applications, and early habit correction.

At Grace Dental Care, we understand that a child's first dental visit shapes their attitude toward dentists for life. Our pediatric team uses behaviour management techniques like "Tell-Show-Do" to explain dental tools in a playful, non-scary way. We provide painless cavity fillings, root canals for kids (pulpectomy), and custom space maintainers, ensuring a positive, tear-free visit.

Indications

Signs You May Need This Treatment

If you identify with any of the following, a clinical consultation is strongly recommended.

Visible Cavities or White SpotsChalky white spots (early decay) or dark brown holes on baby teeth.
Tooth Pain or Crying while EatingChild complaining of toothache, refusing to chew on one side, or crying during meals.
Thumb Sucking / Pacifier HabitChronic sucking habit beyond age 3 that is shifting front teeth forward.
Early Loss of baby ToothA milk tooth lost prematurely due to decay or injury, risking adjacent teeth shifting.
Swollen Gums or Mouth SoreRedness or small pimple-like swelling on gums near baby tooth roots.
Teeth Grinding at NightChild clenching or grinding their teeth during sleep, causing wear.
The Process

Step-by-Step Procedure

What to expect at every stage of your treatment journey.

1
Playful Consultation & Acclimatisation
We welcome the child, show them the chair, and introduce our tools as "water spray" or "tooth tickler" to eliminate fear.

2
Gentle Cleaning & Polish
We clean plaque and soft deposits from the child's teeth using a soft rubber cup and bubblegum-flavoured paste.
⏱ 15 mins

Preventive Fluoride Shield
We apply a highly concentrated fluoride varnish or sealants to deep grooves of back molars to protect teeth from cavities.

For decay, we remove bacteria and place tooth-coloured composite fillings. If decay reaches the nerve, we perform a pulpectomy (child RCT).

If the child has a thumb-sucking habit, we fit a custom, comfortable habit-breaking appliance to protect jaw development.
⏱ 30 mins

Recovery

Aftercare & Recovery Tips

Following these instructions carefully will maximize your treatment success and speed up healing.

1
Supervise Brushing Until Age 8Children lack the dexterity to clean teeth properly. Parents should brush or supervise until age 8.
2
Brush Twice DailyEnsure brushing occurs after breakfast and, most importantly, right before bed.
3
Use Fluoride ToothpasteUse a pea-sized amount of child-friendly fluoride toothpaste (1000ppm) once they can spit.
4
Limit Sticky Sugary SweetsAvoid chocolates, sticky candies, raisins, and juices that stick to teeth. Encourage water.
5
Watch for Numb LipsAfter local anaesthesia, monitor the child so they do not accidentally bite their numb lip or cheek.
6
Visit Every 6 MonthsChildren's teeth decay faster than adults. Regular check-ups spot cavities when they are tiny.
Common Questions

Treatment FAQs

Baby teeth are crucial for several reasons: they allow proper chewing and nutrition, assist in correct speech development, and serve as placeholders for permanent teeth. Early baby tooth loss can lead to severe crowding.
The American Academy of Pediatric Dentistry recommends that a child should have their first dental check-up by their first birthday or when their first tooth erupts. This helps identify early patterns and keeps teeth cavity-free.
Dental sealants are thin, protective plastic coatings applied to the chewing surfaces of back molars. They seal the deep grooves where 90% of cavities start, acting as a shield against food and plaque. They are completely safe.
If a baby tooth is lost early due to decay or injury, a space maintainer is a small metal loop cemented to adjacent teeth. It holds the empty space open, preventing surrounding teeth from drifting and blocking the permanent tooth.
