Conservative Dentistry

Preservation-first care focused on saving your natural teeth — using tooth-coloured composite fillings, inlay restorations, and cosmetic bonding to repair decay and damage early.

Tooth Preservation Focus Tooth-Coloured Composites No Recovery Time Minimally Invasive Care
Overview

About Conservative Dentistry

Conservative dentistry is a branch of dental care focused on diagnosing, preventing, and restoring defects in teeth while preserving as much healthy, natural tooth structure as possible. Instead of extracting teeth or placing extensive crowns, conservative dentistry treats problems early using tooth-coloured composite fillings, dental bonding, and laboratory-fabricated inlays or onlays.

At Grace Dental Care, we follow a strict preservation-first philosophy. We use premium biocompatible materials like resin composites that bond chemically to your enamel, requiring minimal drilling. Our dentist meticulously restores teeth to their natural shape, contour, and color, stopping decay in its tracks and avoiding the need for root canals or extractions.

Indications

Signs You May Need This Treatment

If you identify with any of the following, a clinical consultation is strongly recommended.

Visible Cavities or HolesDark brown, black, or grey spots on teeth indicating active bacterial decay.
Sensitivity to Hot or ColdMild pain or sharp twinges when drinking cold water or hot tea.
Pain when Eating SweetsSugary foods triggering sensitivity, indicating decay reaching the dentine layer.
Food Constantly TrappingFood pieces constantly getting stuck between specific teeth due to a cavity.
Chipped or Fractured EnamelRough edges or cracks on teeth caused by biting hard food.
Rough or Catching FillingAn old silver or composite filling that has cracked, leaked, or is catching floss.
The Process

Step-by-Step Procedure

What to expect at every stage of your treatment journey.

1
Decay Check & Preparation
We locate decay using digital X-rays and tactile exams. The tooth is isolated and decayed enamel is removed gently.
⏱ 15 mins · Visit 1
2
Etching & Adhesive Application
A mild etching gel is applied to create microscopic pores in the enamel, followed by a bonding agent that is cured with a blue UV light.
⏱ 10 mins
3
Layered Composite Filling
Tooth-coloured composite resin is placed in layers, shaped to match natural tooth contours, and hardened using a light-curing gun.
⏱ 15 mins
4
Bite Check & Adjustments
We check your bite using carbon paper to ensure the filling is not too high. We trim down any high points.
⏱ 5 mins
5
Polishing & Sealing
The filling is polished to a high shine, creating a smooth surface that resists stain and plaque build-up.
⏱ 5 mins

Eligibility

Am I a Candidate?

Ideal Candidates

  • Patients with small to moderate cavities
  • Anyone with minor chips or fractures in front or back teeth
  • Patients looking to replace old, dark silver amalgam fillings
  • Those with mild gaps between front teeth wanting bonding

Pre-treatment / Not Suitable if…

  • Large decay extending into the pulp nerve (requires Root Canal Treatment)
  • Tooth structure is mostly broken down (requires a structural dental crown)
  • Severe decay extending deep below the gumline

Recovery

Aftercare & Recovery Tips

Following these instructions carefully will maximize your treatment success and speed up healing.

1
Wait for Numbness to Wear OffDo not eat or drink hot liquids while anaesthetised to prevent biting your tongue or cheek.
2
Mild Sensitivity is NormalYou may feel mild sensitivity to hot or cold for 2–3 days. Desensitising paste helps.
3
Brush and Floss NormallyFillings require the same care as natural teeth. Brush twice daily and floss under margins.
4
Limit Stain-Causing FoodsComposites can stain over years. Limit coffee, tea, and turmeric-heavy foods.
5
Avoid Chewing Very Hard ObjectsDo not bite nails, pens, ice, or bones on the filled tooth to prevent fractures.
6
Schedule 6-Monthly CleaningsProfessional polishing keeps filling margins smooth, preventing plaque trapping.
