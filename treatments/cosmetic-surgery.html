Overview

About Cosmetic Surgery

Cosmetic dental surgery focuses on reshaping gums and dental tissues to improve the proportions and symmetry of your smile. Many people have healthy teeth but feel self-conscious due to an uneven gumline, teeth that look too short, or a "gummy smile" (excessive gum tissue showing when smiling). These issues are corrected through minor surgical procedures like gingival contouring, crown lengthening, and lip repositioning.

At Grace Dental Care, we perform cosmetic gum surgery using advanced soft-tissue lasers. This minimizes bleeding, eliminates the need for stitches, and allows for rapid healing. Dr. Sherin Grace Babu plans every contouring procedure using Digital Smile Design principles, ensuring the gum margins frame your teeth beautifully and complement your facial proportions.

Indications

Signs You May Need This Treatment

If you identify with any of the following, a clinical consultation is strongly recommended.

Gummy SmileShowing more than 3mm of gum tissue when you smile, making your teeth appear abnormally small.
Uneven or Crooked GumlineGums covering some teeth more than others, making teeth look asymmetrical.
Short, Stubby TeethHealthy teeth that look short because they are covered by excess gum and bone tissue.
Dark Gum PatchesMelanin pigment spots on the gums that make the smile look dull (treated with laser ablation).
Hyperactive Upper LipUpper lip pulling up too high when you laugh, exposing excessive gum tissue.
Teeth Shifting borderGum borders shifting asymmetrically following orthodontic treatment.
The Process

Step-by-Step Procedure

What to expect at every stage of your treatment journey.

1
Smile Assessment & DSD Design
We analyze your smile photos and design your ideal gum border digitally, calculating exactly how much gum tissue to contour.
⏱ 30 mins · Visit 1
2
Topical & Local Anaesthesia
A desensitising gel is applied, followed by mild local anaesthetic to make the gum tissue completely numb.
⏱ 10 mins
3
Laser Gum Contouring
A precise diode laser vaporizes excess gum tissue, shaping the margin of each tooth. The laser seals the gums, resulting in zero bleeding.
⏱ 30 mins
4
Aesthetic Crown Lengthening
If needed, a tiny layer of bone border is reshaped underneath the gum to ensure the tooth remains long permanently.
⏱ 30 mins
5
Polishing & Care Review
The gums are cleaned, the bite is verified, and you receive instructions for home care. No stitches are required for laser contouring.
⏱ 15 mins

Eligibility

Am I a Candidate?

Ideal Candidates

  • Adults unhappy with showing too much gum when smiling
  • Patients with uneven, wavy gum borders across their teeth
  • Individuals needing crown lengthening before getting porcelain crowns
  • Patients with dark gum pigmentation wanting pink gums

Pre-treatment / Not Suitable if…

  • Active gum disease or bleeding gums (must be treated with scaling first)
  • Uncontrolled systemic conditions affecting healing (e.g., uncontrolled diabetes)
  • Extremely thin gums with exposed tooth roots

Recovery

Aftercare & Recovery Tips

Following these instructions carefully will maximize your treatment success and speed up healing.

1
Soft, Non-Spicy DietEat soft, cool foods for 3 days. Avoid hot, spicy, or crunchy foods that can irritate healing gums.
2
Gentle Saline RinsesRinse gently with warm salt water 3 times a day starting 24 hours after the procedure.
3
Careful BrushingDo not brush the gum borders directly for the first 3 days. Use a cotton swab to clean teeth.
4
Use Prescribed OintmentApply the soothing, antimicrobial gel to the gum border as prescribed.
5
Avoid Touching GumsDo not pick at, scrape, or touch the healing gum tissues with your fingers or tongue.
6
Do Not SmokeSmoking halts gum healing and increases the risk of post-operative infection.
