Overview

What Is Full Mouth Rehabilitation?

Full Mouth Rehabilitation (FMR), also known as full mouth reconstruction, is a comprehensive dental treatment plan that restores all teeth in both the upper and lower jaws simultaneously. It is designed for patients with extensive tooth wear, multiple missing or broken teeth, decayed teeth, and severe bite collapse. Rather than fixing teeth individually, FMR looks at the entire oral system as a whole.

At Grace Dental Care, we plan your FMR using digital scans, X-rays, and bite simulators. Our specialists combine crowns, bridges, dental implants, veneers, and root canals in a phased, coordinated approach. The goal is to establish a correct bite, restore normal chewing force, eliminate jaw joint pain (TMJ), and create a beautiful, natural-looking smile that lasts.

Indications

Signs You May Need This Treatment

If you identify with any of the following, a clinical consultation is strongly recommended.

Multiple Missing TeethGaps across several areas of the mouth making chewing difficult and painful.
Severely Worn-Down TeethTeeth ground down to short stumps due to chronic bruxism (clenching) or acid erosion.
Frequent Jaw Joint (TMJ) PainChronic jaw clicking, headaches, or muscle pain caused by a collapsed bite.
Loose or Shifting TeethAdvanced decay or gum disease causing multiple teeth to become unstable.
Sunken Facial AppearanceLoss of tooth height and volume causing cheeks and lips to sink, accelerating ageing.
Inability to Eat ComfortablyAvoiding hard, fibrous, or hot/cold foods because biting is painful or inefficient.
The Process

Step-by-Step Procedure

What to expect at every stage of your treatment journey.

1
Multidisciplinary Assessment & Scans
Our prosthodontist, implantologist, and endodontist evaluate your mouth. We take CBCT 3D scans, digital photos, and bite records.
⏱ 90 mins · Visit 1
2
Digital Treatment Design & Mock-Up
We design your new teeth and bite digitally. A diagnostic mock-up (wax-up) is created so you can see and preview the proposed shape and height.
⏱ 60 mins · Visit 2
3
Preparatory Phase (RCTs, Implants, Extractions)
We build the foundation: perform necessary root canals, place dental implants for missing roots, and extract unsavable teeth.
⏱ Phased over 1–3 visits
4
Tooth Preparation & Temporaries
Remaining teeth are prepared for crowns or veneers. Custom temporary restorations are fitted to test the new bite and appearance.
⏱ 3–4 hours · Visit 5
5
Final Bonding & Custom Fitting
Your permanent zirconia or E.max porcelain crowns, bridges, and veneers are bonded. The bite is verified with micro-precision.
⏱ 3–4 hours · Final Visit

Eligibility

Am I a Candidate?

Ideal Candidates

  • Patients with severe wear due to grinding or acid reflux
  • Individuals with multiple broken, missing, or decayed teeth
  • Patients with collapsed bite height causing TMJ issues
  • Seniors looking to restore complete dental health and aesthetics

Pre-treatment / Not Suitable if…

  • Uncontrolled systemic diseases (diabetes, bleeding disorders)
  • Severe, untreated periodontal disease (gums must be stabilised first)
  • Extremely poor bone density (requires extensive bone grafting first)

Recovery

Aftercare & Recovery Tips

Following these instructions carefully will maximize your treatment success and speed up healing.

1
Wear a Protective Night GuardIf you grind your teeth, a custom night guard is mandatory to protect your new crowns.
2
Soft Diet InitiallyEat soft foods for 2 weeks after final cementation to allow jaw muscles to adapt to the new bite.
3
Floss Daily with SuperflossUse specialised floss to clean under bridges and around dental implants.
4
Use an Intraoral WaterpikA water flosser is highly recommended to clean plaque from hard-to-reach prosthesis borders.
5
Attend Review AppointmentsVisit us 1 month and 3 months after completion to verify the bite remains perfect.
6
Biannual Professional ScalingProfessional cleaning every 6 months ensures the health of the bone and gums supporting restorations.
