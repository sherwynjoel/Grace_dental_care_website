Overview

About Sports Dentistry

Sports dentistry deals with the prevention and treatment of dental injuries and related oral diseases associated with sports and athletic participation. Dental trauma in sports is highly common, ranging from chipped or knocked-out teeth to jaw fractures and concussion risks. The primary preventive tool is a custom-fitted sports mouthguard, which absorbs impact shock, protecting teeth, lips, and jaws.

At Grace Dental Care, we create pressure-laminated, custom-fit sports mouthguards for athletes of all levels (cricket, basketball, martial arts, football). Unlike store-bought boil-and-bite mouthguards that slip and restrict breathing, our custom guards fit snugly over your teeth, allowing you to breathe, speak, and drink easily. We also provide immediate emergency care for knocked-out or fractured teeth.

Recovery

Aftercare & Recovery Tips

Following these instructions carefully will maximize your treatment success and speed up healing.

1
Wash with Cold WaterRinse your mouthguard with cold water or mouthwash before and after every use.
2
Clean with a Soft BrushGently clean the guard occasionally using a soft-bristled brush and mild soap. No toothpaste.
3
Keep Away from Hot HeatNever leave the mouthguard in hot water, direct sunlight, or a hot car, which will melt and warp it.
4
Store in the CaseAlways store the mouthguard in its ventilated plastic box to keep it dry and prevent bacterial growth.
5
Do Not Chew on the GuardChewing on the borders will damage its fit and compromise its protective shock-absorption.
6
Inspect for Wear YearlyBring your mouthguard to your checkup. Replace it if it shows cracks, tears, or becomes loose.
