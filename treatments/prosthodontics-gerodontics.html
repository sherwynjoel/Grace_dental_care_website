Overview

About Prosthodontics & Gerodontics

Prosthodontics is the dental specialty focused on the design, fabrication, and fitting of artificial replacements for teeth and other oral structures. Gerodontics is the delivery of dental care to older adults, addressing the physical, medical, and psychological changes associated with ageing. This dual care pathway focuses on restoring chewing comfort, speech clarity, and nutritional health.

At Grace Dental Care, our cosmetic dentist and prosthodontist Dr. Sherin Grace Babu MDS specialises in senior dental care. We design a range of prostheses including implant-supported overdentures, cast partial dentures, and CAD/CAM zirconia bridges. We take into consideration medical conditions (like diabetes or hypertension) to deliver safe, minimally invasive, and comfortable treatments.

Indications

Signs You May Need This Treatment

If you identify with any of the following, a clinical consultation is strongly recommended.

Severe Tooth Loss / EdentulousMissing most or all teeth, causing chewing failure and slurred speech.
Severely Worn-Down BiteFlat, short teeth due to decades of grinding, causing jaw joint discomfort.
Dry Mouth (Xerostomia)Reduced saliva flow due to age or medications, causing gum soreness and decay.
Loose, Uncomfortable DenturesDentures that slip out of place, click, or cause painful gum ulcers.
Inability to Chew Hard FoodsStruggling to eat nutritious foods like apples, meat, or vegetables.
Sunken Lips and CheeksFacial collapse due to tooth and bone loss, accelerating ageing wrinkles.

Recovery

Aftercare & Recovery Tips

Following these instructions carefully will maximize your treatment success and speed up healing.

1
Clean Dentures After EatingRemove and wash dentures under running water after meals to clear food debris.
2
Brush Gums and TongueUse a soft-bristled brush to clean gums, palate, and tongue daily to stimulate blood circulation.
3
Soak Prosthetics OvernightKeep removable dentures in water at night to prevent warping or drying.
4
Manage Dry MouthSip water frequently, chew sugar-free gums, or use salivary substitutes if dry mouth persists.
5
Do Not Wear Dentures to SleepGive gums 6–8 hours of rest daily to prevent fungal infections (denture stomatitis).
6
Annual Bone Ridge ChecksVisit us yearly. Gums naturally shrink, and dentures may require relining for a snug fit.
