Sterilisation & Safety

Your health and safety are our highest priority. Every instrument, surface, and procedure follows strict infection control protocols — so you can focus on your smile, not your safety.

Our Commitment

Why Sterilisation
Matters

Cross-infection control is the cornerstone of safe dental practice. Dental procedures involve contact with blood, saliva, and oral tissues — which is why every instrument must be properly sterilised between patients, without exception.

At Grace Dental Care, we go beyond the minimum standards required by the Indian Dental Association (IDA). Our protocols are modelled on international best practices used by leading clinics in Europe and North America.

We are proud to say that in over a decade of practice, we have maintained a zero infection incident record — and we intend to keep it that way.

Our Process

The 6-Step
Sterilisation Protocol

Every reusable instrument goes through all six steps before it touches another patient. No shortcuts, ever.

01

Pre-Cleaning Rinse

Instruments are immediately submerged in enzymatic solution after use to break down organic matter and prevent debris from drying.

02

Ultrasonic Cleaning

An ultrasonic cleaner uses high-frequency sound waves to dislodge all remaining contaminants from every crevice of the instrument.

03

Rinse & Inspection

Each instrument is rinsed thoroughly and visually inspected under magnification. Any item that doesn't pass inspection is discarded.

04

Packaging & Sealing

Clean instruments are sealed in sterilisation pouches with built-in chemical indicators that confirm successful sterilisation.

05

Autoclave Sterilisation

Sealed packs are sterilised in our Class B autoclave at 134°C and 2.1 bar pressure for 18 minutes — killing all bacteria, viruses, and spores.

06

Sealed Storage

Sterile packs are stored in a closed, dry cabinet and opened only in front of the patient immediately before the procedure begins.

Infection Control

Beyond Instruments —
Full Infection Control

Single-Use Disposables

Needles, gloves, masks, suction tips, saliva ejectors, and air/water syringe tips are brand new for every patient and disposed of immediately after use.

Surface Disinfection

All clinical surfaces — dental chairs, brackets, light handles, and countertops — are disinfected with hospital-grade disinfectant spray and barrier films between every patient.

Staff PPE Protocol

All clinical team members wear fresh gloves, masks, eye protection, and clinical gowns for every patient. Hand hygiene is performed before gloving and after glove removal.

Water Line Purging

Dental unit water lines are flushed for 2 minutes at the start of every day and 30 seconds before each patient to prevent biofilm accumulation.

Biomedical Waste Disposal

All clinical waste is segregated by category (sharps, infectious, general) and disposed of through a licensed biomedical waste management company, as required by regulations.

HEPA Air Filtration

Our clinic uses high-efficiency air purifiers to reduce aerosol contamination in the treatment area, providing a consistently clean clinical environment for staff and patients.
