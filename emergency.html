Recognise the Signs

Is This a
Dental Emergency?

If you're experiencing any of the following, don't wait — call us immediately. Timely treatment can mean the difference between saving and losing a tooth.

Severe Toothache

Throbbing, persistent pain that won't respond to over-the-counter relief. Could signal infection or nerve damage.

Chipped or Broken Tooth

From trauma or an accident — even a small chip can expose sensitive dentine and worsen quickly without treatment.

Knocked-Out Tooth

Reattachment is possible within 1 hour. Store the tooth in milk and call us immediately for the best outcome.

Lost Crown or Filling

Exposed nerve, sharp edges, and sudden sensitivity — an unprotected tooth needs to be restored without delay.

Dental Abscess

Facial swelling, fever, or a bad taste in your mouth — a dental abscess is a serious infection requiring urgent care.

Gum Bleeding

Heavy, persistent bleeding from the gums that doesn't stop within minutes — especially after an injury — needs immediate attention.

Jaw Pain or Lockjaw

Difficulty opening or closing your mouth, clicking, or jaw locking can indicate joint or muscle problems needing urgent assessment.

Broken Braces / Wire

A wire poking your cheek or a bracket that has come off can cause cuts and delay treatment — call us for same-day adjustment.

Act Fast

What To Do
Right Now

Follow these steps while you make your way to us. Every minute matters in a dental emergency.

Call Us Immediately

Call +91 80153 29529. We'll assess urgency and book you in the same day. Don't wait to see if it gets better.

Handle With Care

For a knocked-out tooth: hold by the crown only, rinse gently, and store in milk or between cheek and gum. Never scrub the root.

Manage Pain

Apply a cold compress to the outside of your cheek in 10-minute intervals. Avoid aspirin directly on gum tissue — it can burn.

Get Here Fast

We reserve emergency slots every day. Mon–Sat, 9 AM–8 PM. Our clinic is on 100 Feet Road, Kovaipudur — ground floor, easy parking.
