Dental Implant
Journey Timeline

From consultation to your permanent smile — here is exactly what the implant process looks like, step by step.

Step 01

Consultation & Scanning

Comprehensive oral exam, digital X-rays, and 3D CBCT scan to assess bone density and plan implant placement precisely.

Duration: 1 visit · 45–60 mins

01
02

Step 02

Implant Placement Surgery

The titanium implant post is placed into the jawbone under local anaesthesia. Most patients report minimal discomfort — just pressure.

Duration: 1–2 hours · Under local anaesthesia

Step 03

Osseointegration Healing

The implant fuses with your jawbone over 8–12 weeks. You wear a temporary crown and continue daily activities normally.

Duration: 8–12 weeks healing

03
04

Step 04

Abutment & Crown Fitting

The abutment connector is placed, impressions are taken, and your custom zirconia crown is fabricated and fitted permanently.

Duration: 2 visits · 1 week apart

Step 05

Review & Lifetime Care

A final review ensures perfect fit, bite, and aesthetics. With good oral hygiene, your implant can last a lifetime.

Duration: 1 visit · 30 mins

Frequently Asked

Your Questions,
Answered

Modern dentistry has come a long way. With effective local anaesthesia, most procedures are completely pain-free. At Grace Dental Care, we take dental anxiety seriously and ensure every patient is fully comfortable before we begin any treatment.
With proper care, dental implants can last a lifetime. The titanium post integrates with your jawbone permanently. The crown on top typically lasts 15–25 years before needing replacement. Good oral hygiene and regular check-ups are key to implant longevity.
It depends on the complexity of your case. A simple whitening or bonding may be done in one visit, while veneers or a full smile makeover typically requires 2–4 visits. We use Digital Smile Design so you can preview your results before any work begins.
For most cases, yes. Clear aligners are highly effective for mild to moderate misalignment. For more complex bite issues, traditional braces may be recommended. Our orthodontist will assess your case and recommend the best option for your specific needs and lifestyle.
We recommend a check-up and cleaning every 6 months for most patients. If you have active gum disease, recent dental work, or are prone to cavities, more frequent visits (every 3–4 months) may be advised. Early detection always leads to simpler, less costly treatment.
Absolutely. Our pediatric dentistry team specialises in treating children from infancy through adolescence. We use child-friendly techniques and a gentle approach to make dental visits a positive experience that builds lifelong healthy habits.
Root canal treatment removes infected or inflamed pulp tissue from inside the tooth, relieves pain, and saves the natural tooth. With modern anaesthesia and rotary instruments, the procedure is no more uncomfortable than a routine filling. Most patients report the anticipation is far worse than the experience — and pain relief is typically felt within 24–48 hours of treatment.
Professional whitening results typically last 1–2 years depending on lifestyle habits such as coffee, tea, red wine, and smoking. Touch-up sessions every 6–12 months keep your shade bright. We also provide custom home whitening trays so you can maintain results between clinic visits.
A veneer is a thin porcelain shell bonded to the front surface of a tooth — ideal for cosmetic corrections to visible front teeth with minimal tooth reduction. A crown encases the entire tooth and is used when the tooth is significantly damaged, decayed, or weakened after a root canal. Our prosthodontist will recommend the right option based on your specific clinical situation.
Routine check-ups and cleanings are safe and actually recommended during pregnancy, as hormonal changes increase gum sensitivity and the risk of gum disease. Elective cosmetic treatments are best postponed until after delivery. X-rays are avoided unless absolutely necessary, and only pregnancy-safe medications are used. Always inform our team if you are or may be pregnant.
Dental implants require the same care as natural teeth — brush twice daily with a soft-bristled brush, floss daily, and attend professional cleanings every 6 months. Avoid biting hard objects and do not smoke, as it impairs healing and long-term success. An ultrasonic flosser (Waterpik) is especially effective for cleaning around implant bases. With proper care, implants can last a lifetime.
Full Mouth Rehabilitation (FMR) is a comprehensive treatment plan that addresses all teeth simultaneously — correcting the bite, restoring worn or damaged teeth, and improving aesthetics and function. It typically combines crowns, veneers, implants, gum therapy, and orthodontics. Treatment is carefully phased over multiple visits using digital planning tools, so you know exactly what to expect at every stage.
